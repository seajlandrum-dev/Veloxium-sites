Our Roots

We are a non-profit dedicated to greening our city and empowering its residents.

Our Mission & Vision

Our mission is to cultivate healthier communities by creating and sustaining urban green spaces, fostering environmental education, and ensuring equitable access to fresh, locally-grown food. We envision a city where every neighborhood is connected through a network of thriving gardens that empower residents and nurture a healthier planet for generations to come.


Founded in 2018, The Bloom Initiative started as a small group of neighbors wanting to convert a vacant lot into a shared garden. Today, we manage over a dozen community gardens and educational programs across the city, all powered by dedicated volunteers and community partners like you.

Our Programs

From garden plots to educational workshops, we offer several ways to help our community grow.

Community Roots Gardens

We establish and support shared garden plots across the city. Members can grow their own fresh produce, share harvests, and connect with neighbors in a beautiful, productive setting.

Seedling Stewards Education

Our workshops for children and adults cover everything from basic botany and composting to water conservation and urban farming techniques. We believe education is key to sustainable change.

Urban Orchard Project

In partnership with city parks, we plant and maintain fruit-bearing trees in public spaces. These orchards provide free, healthy snacks for the community and enhance local biodiversity.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need my own tools or any gardening experience to volunteer?

Not at all! We welcome volunteers of all skill levels. We provide all the necessary tools and gloves, and our site leaders are always on hand to guide you. All you need to bring is a positive attitude and a willingness to learn.

How can I get a plot in a Community Roots Garden?

Plots are assigned on a first-come, first-served basis each spring. You can sign up for our mailing list on the "Get Involved" page to be notified when plot registration opens. A small annual fee is required to cover water and tool maintenance costs.

Are your programs and workshops free?

Yes, the vast majority of our community workshops and events are completely free and open to the public, thanks to generous donations and grants. Some specialized, multi-session courses may have a small materials fee.

What do my donations support?

100% of your donation goes directly toward our programs. Donations fund the purchase of seeds, soil, compost, gardening tools, educational materials for our workshops, and maintenance for our public urban orchards.
