Meet King Adonis

At eight years old, King Adonis is more than just a name—it's a mindset. He is on a structured journey of self-discovery, embracing challenges with focus and enthusiasm. This website, managed by his parents, documents his progress and shares the principles we use to guide him.

We believe in fostering natural curiosity with a framework of discipline. From the mat in his Judo dojo to the chessboard, every activity is an opportunity to learn about strategy, respect, and resilience.

Core Strengths We're Nurturing:

  • Unwavering Focus
  • Creative Problem-Solving
  • Disciplined Execution
  • Empathetic Leadership

Activities & Achievements

Where discipline meets passion. Here's a look at the core activities shaping his development.

Judo: The Gentle Way

Currently a Yellow Belt, King is learning respect, control, and the art of kuzushi (off-balancing). His favorite throw is the O Goshi (Major Hip Throw).

Chess: The Mind's Arena

As a member of the school's chess club, he is mastering openings and developing long-term strategies. He believes every piece is a part of a team.

Coding: Building Worlds

Starting with Scratch and now moving onto basic Python concepts. He loves creating simple games and animations, turning ideas into interactive realities.

Learning Every Day

Growth isn't an accident. It's the result of consistent effort and the right resources.

📖

The 30-Minute Reading Rule

Every day, without fail, we dedicate 30 minutes to reading. His current favorites are "The Magic Treehouse" series and the "Who Was?" biographies, which spark his curiosity about history and great figures.

🧠

Digital Brain Training

We leverage technology for learning. Platforms like Prodigy Math make math feel like a game, while Khan Kids Academy provides a broad base of engaging lessons across various subjects. It's about smart screen time.

💬

Weekly 'Big Question' Debates

To foster critical thinking and communication, we have weekly family debates on a "big question," such as "Is it better to be strong or smart?" This teaches him to form and articulate his own opinions respectfully.

June 01, 2024 | Judo

The Power of Ukemi: Learning to Fall

This week in Judo was all about Ukemi (break-falls). The biggest lesson wasn't about fighting, but about how to fall safely and get back up instantly. A powerful metaphor for resilience that we've been discussing all week.

May 25, 2024 | Coding

First Python 'Hello, World!' Moment

A huge milestone! King wrote his first line of Python code this weekend: print("Hello, Adonis"). The look of pure joy and power on his face seeing the computer follow his command was priceless. The journey begins!

May 18, 2024 | Life Skills

The Responsibility Chart: Beyond Chores

We've upgraded our chore chart to a 'Responsibility Chart'. It includes tasks like 'Prepare your own lunch' and 'Lead a family meeting'. It's about fostering ownership, not just completing tasks for a reward.
