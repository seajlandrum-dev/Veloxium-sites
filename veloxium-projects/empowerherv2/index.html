More Than a Mission, It's a Movement

Our Mission: Empowerment Through Duality

Our mission is to empower women and girls by holistically developing their physical confidence and intellectual capabilities. We believe that the resilience learned on the mats directly translates to the problem-solving and ethical reasoning required in the tech world. We provide a safe, supportive space where strength and smarts are not mutually exclusive, but mutually reinforcing.

Our Values

  • Resilience: We embrace challenges on and off the mat as opportunities for growth.
  • Community: We foster a sisterhood built on mutual respect, support, and collaboration.
  • Integrity: We champion responsible innovation and ethical conduct in all our pursuits.
  • Courage: We empower our members to step outside their comfort zones and claim their space.

Two Paths, One Purpose

We offer two core programs designed to build a complete foundation for personal and professional growth. Each program can be taken individually, but their true power is unlocked when experienced together.

The Mat is Our Canvas

Our Brazilian Jiu Jitsu program teaches self-defense, physical fitness, and mental fortitude. It's a journey of self-discovery through movement, strategy, and connection with others.

Code with Conscience

Our Responsible AI program demystifies artificial intelligence, focusing on ethical considerations, practical applications, and building a future where technology serves humanity equitably.

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu: The Gentle Art of Strength

Often called "human chess," BJJ is a ground-based martial art that uses leverage and technique, not just size and strength, to control an opponent. It's a perfect discipline for learning how to solve problems under pressure and build unshakable self-confidence.

Program Overview

Our curriculum is designed for all levels, from absolute beginners to experienced practitioners. We offer women-only classes that provide a comfortable and focused learning environment.

Expert Instructors

Led by Head Instructor and founder, Maria Costa, a BJJ black belt dedicated to creating a positive and empowering atmosphere. All our instructors are certified and passionate about teaching.

Key Benefits

Develop practical self-defense skills, improve physical fitness and body awareness, boost mental resilience, reduce stress, and join a powerful, supportive community.

Responsible AI: Shaping a Smarter Future

Artificial Intelligence is changing the world. Our "Code with Conscience" program ensures that our members are not just consumers of this technology, but are equipped to be its architects, critics, and ethical guardians.

Curriculum Overview

Our project-based curriculum covers AI fundamentals, machine learning concepts, data literacy, and crucially, AI ethics. No prior coding experience is required for our introductory workshops.

Industry Mentors

Workshops are led by women in tech from leading companies. Our lead curriculum developer, Dr. Anya Sharma, is a data scientist specializing in algorithmic fairness and transparency.

Success Stories

Graduates have gone on to pursue tech degrees, secure internships, and even launch their own small-scale AI projects for social good. They become advocates for ethical tech in their communities.

Frequently Asked Questions

Responsible AI is the practice of designing, developing, and deploying artificial intelligence with good intention to empower employees and businesses, and fairly impact customers and society. We teach it because as AI becomes more powerful, it's crucial that its creators understand the ethical implications, potential for bias, and its societal impact to build a more equitable future.

Our programs are open to all who identify as women and girls, with no upper age limit. We believe empowerment is a lifelong journey. We have specific cohorts for different age groups (e.g., teens and adults) to ensure the content is relevant, but our community is intergenerational.

For your first trial class, comfortable athletic clothing like leggings and a t-shirt is perfectly fine. We recommend clothes without zippers or pockets for safety. If you decide to join, you will need a BJJ uniform called a "gi," and we can help you find an affordable one. We also have loaner gis available for your first few classes.

Donations directly fund our mission. They are used to subsidize program fees for low-income participants, purchase training equipment and gis, maintain our training facility, and provide laptops and software for our AI workshops.
